# Music & Audio Cues

## Audio Design for Video Production

### Purpose
This document outlines all music tracks, sound effects, and audio cues needed throughout the video to enhance the narrative and emotional impact.

---

## Overall Audio Strategy
- **Tone:** Inspiring, tech-forward, energetic
- **Style:** Modern electronic with orchestral elements
- **Pace:** Dynamic, matching scene energy
- **Volume:** Background music at -20dB to -15dB to not overpower dialogue

## Dialogue & Voiceover

### Recording Specifications
- **Format:** 48kHz, 24-bit WAV
- **Microphone:** Condenser, cardioid pattern
- **Environment:** Treated room, minimal reverb
- **Processing:** De-ess, gentle compression, EQ

### Voice Treatment by Section
1. **Opening (Heavy Mic):** Natural, slightly strained
2. **Pivot Decision:** Confident, determined
3. **Technical Sections:** Clear, educational
4. **Family Section:** Warm, relatable
5. **Demo Sections:** Professional, guide-like
6. **Conclusion:** Inspirational, inviting

### Dialogue Cleanup Needed
- [ ] Remove breath noises (keep natural ones)
- [ ] Remove plosives
- [ ] Consistent volume levels
- [ ] Room tone matching between takes
- [ ] Crossfade between cuts

## Audio Mix Notes

### Dialogue Priority
- Dialogue always primary (loudest element)
- Music ducks to -22dB during speech
- Sound effects at -20dB to -25dB

### Frequency Management
- **Dialogue:** Clear 2kHz-5kHz for intelligibility
- **Music:** Roll off below 100Hz if dialogue present
- **SFX:** Find pocket in frequency spectrum

### Dynamic Range
- Peak levels at -6dB to -3dB
- Average loudness: -16 LUFS for YouTube
- True peak limiting at -1dB

### Export Specifications
- **Master Mix:** 48kHz, 24-bit WAV
- **YouTube Upload:** 48kHz, 16-bit AAC (320kbps)
- **Stems Export:** Individual tracks for remixing
- **Include:** Tone at head (1kHz, -20dB, 10 seconds)
